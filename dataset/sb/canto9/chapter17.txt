CHAPTER SEVENTEEN
The Dynasties of the Sons of Pururava

ayu, the eldest son of Pururava, had five sons. This chapter describes the dynasties of four of them, beginning with Kshatravrddha.

ayu, the son of Pururava, had five sons — Nahusha, Kshatravrddha, Raji, Rabha and Anena. The son of Kshatravrddha was Suhotra, who had three sons, named KaShya, KuSha and Grtsamada. The son of Grtsamada was Shunaka, and his son was Shaunaka. The son of KaShya was KaShi. From KaShi came the sons and grandsons known as Rashtra, Dirghatama and then Dhanvantari, who was the inaugurator of medical science and was a ShaktyaveSha incarnation of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Vasudeva. The descendants of Dhanvantari were Ketuman, Bhimaratha, Divodasa and Dyuman, who was also known as Pratardana, Shatrujit, Vatsa, rtadhvaja and KuvalayaShva. The son of Dyuman was Alarka, who reigned over the kingdom for many, many years. Following in the dynasty of Alarka were Santati, Sunitha, Niketana, Dharmaketu, Satyaketu, Dhrshtaketu, Sukumara, Vitihotra, Bharga and Bhargabhumi. All of them belonged to the dynasty of KaShi, the descendant of Kshatravrddha.

The son of Rabha was Rabhasa, and his son was Gambhira. Gambhira's son was Akriya, and from Akriya came Brahmavit. The son of Anena was Shuddha, and his son was Shuci. The son of Shuci was Citrakrt, whose son was Shantaraja. Raji had five hundred sons, all of extraordinary strength. Raji was personally very powerful and was given the kingdom of heaven by Lord Indra. Later, after Raji's death, when the sons of Raji refused to return the kingdom to Indra, by Brhaspati's arrangement they became unintelligent, and Lord Indra conquered them.

The grandson of Kshatravrddha named KuSha gave birth to a son named Prati. From Prati came Sanjaya; from Sanjaya, Jaya; from Jaya, Krta; and from Krta, Haryabala. The son of Haryabala was Sahadeva; the son of Sahadeva, Hina; the son of Hina, Jayasena; the son of Jayasena, Sankrti; and the son of Sankrti, Jaya.

Texts 1-3

Translation
Shukadeva Gosvami said: From Pururava came a son named ayu, whose very powerful sons were Nahusha, Kshatravrddha, Raji, Rabha and Anena. O Maharaja Parikshit, now hear about the dynasty of Kshatravrddha. Kshatravrddha's son was Suhotra, who had three sons, named KaShya, KuSha and Grtsamada. From Grtsamada came Shunaka, and from him came Shaunaka, the great saint, the best of those conversant with the rg Veda.

Text 4

Translation
The son of KaShya was KaShi, and his son was Rashtra, the father of Dirghatama. Dirghatama had a son named Dhanvantari, who was the inaugurator of the medical science and an incarnation of Lord Vasudeva, the enjoyer of the results of sacrifices. One who remembers the name of Dhanvantari can be released from all disease.

Text 5

Translation
The son of Dhanvantari was Ketuman, and his son was Bhimaratha. The son of Bhimaratha was Divodasa, and the son of Divodasa was Dyuman, also known as Pratardana.

Text 6

Translation
Dyuman was also known as Shatrujit, Vatsa, rtadhvaja and KuvalayaShva. From him were born Alarka and other sons.

Text 7

Translation
Alarka, the son of Dyuman, reigned over the earth for sixty-six thousand years, my dear King Parikshit. No one other than him has reigned over the earth for so long as a young man.

Text 8

Translation
From Alarka came a son named Santati, and his son was Sunitha. The son of Sunitha was Niketana, the son of Niketana was Dharmaketu, and the son of Dharmaketu was Satyaketu.

Text 9

Translation
O King Parikshit, from Satyaketu came a son named Dhrshtaketu, and from Dhrshtaketu came Sukumara, the emperor of the entire world. From Sukumara came a son named Vitihotra; from Vitihotra, Bharga; and from Bharga, Bhargabhumi.

Text 10

Translation
O Maharaja Parikshit, all of these kings were descendants of KaShi, and they could also be called descendants of Kshatravrddha. The son of Rabha was Rabhasa, from Rabhasa came Gambhira, and from Gambhira came a son named Akriya.

Text 11

Translation
The son of Akriya was known as Brahmavit, O King. Now hear about the descendants of Anena. From Anena came a son named Shuddha, and his son was Shuci. The son of Shuci was Dharmasarathi, also called Citrakrt.

Text 12

Translation
From Citrakrt was born a son named Shantaraja, a self-realized soul who performed all kinds of Vedic ritualistic ceremonies and therefore did not beget any progeny. The sons of Raji were five hundred, all very powerful.

Text 13

Translation
On the request of the demigods, Raji killed the demons and thus returned the kingdom of heaven to Lord Indra. But Indra, fearing such demons as Prahlada, returned the kingdom of heaven to Raji and surrendered himself at Raji's lotus feet.

Text 14

Translation
Upon Raji's death, Indra begged Raji's sons for the return of the heavenly planet. They did not return it, however, although they agreed to return Indra's shares in ritualistic ceremonies.

Purport
Raji conquered the kingdom of heaven, and therefore when Indra, the heavenly king, begged Raji's sons to return it, they refused. Because they had not taken the heavenly kingdom from Indra but had inherited it from their father, they considered it their paternal property. Why then should they return it to the demigods?

Text 15

Translation
Thereafter, Brhaspati, the spiritual master of the demigods, offered oblations in the fire so that the sons of Raji would fall from moral principles. When they fell, Lord Indra killed them easily because of their degradation. Not a single one of them remained alive.

Text 16

Translation
From KuSha, the grandson of Kshatravrddha, was born a son named Prati. The son of Prati was Sanjaya, and the son of Sanjaya was Jaya. From Jaya, Krta was born, and from Krta, King Haryabala.

Text 17

Translation
From Haryabala came a son named Sahadeva, and from Sahadeva came Hina. The son of Hina was Jayasena, and the son of Jayasena was Sankrti. The son of Sankrti was the powerful and expert fighter named Jaya. These kings were the members of the Kshatravrddha dynasty. Now let me describe to you the dynasty of Nahusha.

Purport
Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Ninth Canto, Seventeenth Chapter, of the Shrimad-Bhagavatam, entitled “The Dynasties of the Sons of Pururava.”
