CHAPTER SEVEN
The Descendants of King Mandhata

In this chapter the descendants of King Mandhata are described, and in this connection the histories of Purukutsa and HariShcandra are also given.

The most prominent son of Mandhata was Ambarisha, his son was YauvanaShva, and YauvanaShva's son was Harita. These three personalities were the best in the dynasty of Mandhata. Purukutsa, another son of Mandhata, married the sister of the snakes (sarpa-gana) named Narmada. The son of Purukutsa was Trasaddasyu, whose son was Anaranya. Anaranya's son was HaryaShva, HaryaShva's son was Praruna, Praruna's son was Tribandhana, and Tribandhana's son was Satyavrata, also known as TriShanku. When TriShanku kidnapped the daughter of a brahmana, his father cursed him for this sinful act, and TriShanku became a candala, worse than a Shudra. Later, by the influence of ViShvamitra, he was brought to the heavenly planets, but by the influence of the demigods he fell back downward. He was stopped in his fall, however, by the influence of ViShvamitra. The son of TriShanku was HariShcandra. HariShcandra once performed a Rajasuya-yajna, but ViShvamitra cunningly took all of HariShcandra's possessions as a dakshina contribution and chastised HariShcandra in various ways. Because of this, a quarrel arose between ViShvamitra and Vasishtha. HariShcandra had no sons, but on the advice of Narada he worshiped Varuna and in this way got a son named Rohita. HariShcandra promised that Rohita would be used to perform a Varuna-yajna. Varuna reminded HariShcandra repeatedly about this yajna, but the King, because of affection for his son, gave various arguments to avoid sacrificing him. Thus time passed, and gradually the son grew up. To safeguard his life, the boy then took bow and arrows in hand and went to the forest. Meanwhile, at home, HariShcandra suffered from dropsy because of an attack from Varuna. When Rohita received the news that his father was suffering, he wanted to return to the capital, but King Indra prevented him from doing so. Following the instructions of Indra, Rohita lived in the forest for six years and then returned home. Rohita purchased ShunahShepha, the second son of Ajigarta, and gave him to his father, HariShcandra, as the sacrificial animal. In this way, the sacrifice was performed, Varuna and the other demigods were pacified, and HariShcandra was freed from disease. In this sacrifice, ViShvamitra was the hota priest, Jamadagni was the adhvaryu, Vasishtha was the brahma, and Ayasya was the udgata. King Indra, being very satisfied by the sacrifice, gave HariShcandra a golden chariot, and ViShvamitra gave him transcendental knowledge. Thus Shukadeva Gosvami describes how HariShcandra achieved perfection.

Text 1

Translation
Shukadeva Gosvami said: The most prominent among the sons of Mandhata was he who is celebrated as Ambarisha. Ambarisha was accepted as son by his grandfather YuvanaShva. Ambarisha's son was YauvanaShva, and YauvanaShva's son was Harita. In Mandhata's dynasty, Ambarisha, Harita and YauvanaShva were very prominent.

Text 2

Translation
The serpent brothers of Narmada gave Narmada to Purukutsa. Being sent by Vasuki, she took Purukutsa to the lower region of the universe.

Purport
Before describing the descendants of Purukutsa, the son of Mandhata, Shukadeva Gosvami first describes how Purukutsa was married to Narmada, who was induced to take him to the lower region of the universe.

Text 3

Translation
There in Rasatala, the lower region of the universe, Purukutsa, being empowered by Lord Vishnu, was able to kill all the Gandharvas who deserved to be killed. Purukutsa received the benediction from the serpents that anyone who remembers this history of his being brought by Narmada to the lower region of the universe will be assured of safety from the attack of snakes.

Text 4

Translation
The son of Purukutsa was Trasaddasyu, who was the father of Anaranya. Anaranya's son was HaryaShva, the father of Praruna. Praruna was the father of Tribandhana.

Texts 5-6

Translation
The son of Tribandhana was Satyavrata, who is celebrated by the name TriShanku. Because he kidnapped the daughter of a brahmana when she was being married, his father cursed him to become a candala, lower than a Shudra. Thereafter, by the influence of ViShvamitra, he went to the higher planetary system, the heavenly planets, in his material body, but because of the prowess of the demigods he fell back downward. Nonetheless, by the power of ViShvamitra, he did not fall all the way down; even today he can still be seen hanging in the sky, head downward.

Text 7

Translation
The son of TriShanku was HariShcandra. Because of HariShcandra there was a quarrel between ViShvamitra and Vasishtha, who for many years fought one another, having been transformed into birds.

Purport
ViShvamitra and Vasishtha were always inimical. Formerly, ViShvamitra was a kshatriya, and by undergoing severe austerities he wanted to become a brahmana, but Vasishtha would not agree to accept him. In this way there was always disagreement between the two. Later, however, Vasishtha accepted him because of ViShvamitra's quality of forgiveness. Once HariShcandra performed a yajna for which ViShvamitra was the priest, but ViShvamitra, being angry at HariShcandra, took away all his possessions, claiming them as a contribution of dakshina. Vasishtha, however, did not like this, and therefore a fight arose between Vasishtha and ViShvamitra. The fighting became so severe that each of them cursed the other. One of them said, “May you become a bird,” and the other said, “May you become a duck.” Thus both of them became birds and continued fighting for many years because of HariShcandra. We can see that such a great mystic yogi as Saubhari became a victim of sense gratification, and such great sages as Vasishtha and ViShvamitra became birds. This is the material world. abrahma-bhuvanal lokah punar avartino 'rjuna. Within this material world, or within this universe, however elevated one may be in material qualities, one must suffer the conditions of birth, death, old age and disease (janma-mrtyu jara-vyadhi). Therefore Krishna says that this material world is simply miserable (duhkhalayam aShaShvatam). The Bhagavatam says, padam padam yad vipadam: at every step here there is danger. Therefore, because the Krishna consciousness movement provides the opportunity for the human being to get out of this material world simply by chanting the Hare Krishna mantra, this movement is the greatest benediction in human society.

Text 8

Translation
HariShcandra had no son and was therefore extremely morose. Once, therefore, following the advice of Narada, he took shelter of Varuna and said to him, “My lord, I have no son. Would you kindly give me one?”

Text 9

Translation
O King Parikshit, HariShcandra begged Varuna, “My lord, if a son is born to me, with that son I shall perform a sacrifice for your satisfaction.” When HariShcandra said this, Varuna replied, “Let it be so.” Because of Varuna's benediction, HariShcandra begot a son named Rohita.

Text 10

Translation
Thereafter, when the child was born, Varuna approached HariShcandra and said, “Now you have a son. With this son you can offer me a sacrifice.” In answer to this, HariShcandra said, “After ten days have passed since an animal's birth, the animal becomes fit to be sacrificed.”

Text 11

Translation
After ten days, Varuna came again and said to HariShcandra, “Now you can perform the sacrifice.” HariShcandra replied, “When an animal grows teeth, then it becomes pure enough to be sacrificed.”

Text 12

Translation
When the teeth grew, Varuna came and said to HariShcandra, “Now the animal has grown teeth, and you can perform the sacrifice.” HariShcandra replied, “When all its teeth have fallen out, then it will be fit for sacrifice.”

Text 13

Translation
When the teeth had fallen out, Varuna returned and said to HariShcandra, “Now the animal's teeth have fallen out, and you can perform the sacrifice.” But HariShcandra replied, “When the animal's teeth grow in again, then he will be pure enough to be sacrificed.”

Text 14

Translation
When the teeth grew in again, Varuna came and said to HariShcandra, “Now you can perform the sacrifice.” But HariShcandra then said, “O King, when the sacrificial animal becomes a kshatriya and is able to shield himself to fight with the enemy, then he will be purified.”

Text 15

Translation
HariShcandra was certainly very much attached to his son. Because of this affection, he asked the demigod Varuna to wait. Thus Varuna waited and waited for the time to come.

Text 16

Translation
Rohita could understand that his father intended to offer him as the animal for sacrifice. Therefore, just to save himself from death, he equipped himself with bow and arrows and went to the forest.

Text 17

Translation
When Rohita heard that his father had been attacked by dropsy due to Varuna and that his abdomen had grown very large, he wanted to return to the capital, but King Indra forbade him to do so.

Text 18

Translation
King Indra advised Rohita to travel to different pilgrimage sites and holy places, for such activities are pious indeed. Following this instruction, Rohita went to the forest for one year.

Text 19

Translation
In this way, at the end of the second, third, fourth and fifth years, when Rohita wanted to return to his capital, the King of heaven, Indra, approached him as an old brahmana and forbade him to return, repeating the same words as in the previous year.

Text 20

Translation
Thereafter, in the sixth year, after wandering in the forest, Rohita returned to the capital of his father. He purchased from Ajigarta his second son, named ShunahShepha. Then he offered ShunahShepha to his father, HariShcandra, to be used as the sacrificial animal and offered HariShcandra his respectful obeisances.

Purport
It appears that in those days a man could be purchased for any purpose. HariShcandra was in need of a person to sacrifice as the animal in a yajna and thus fulfill his promise to Varuna, and a man was purchased from another man for this purpose. Millions of years ago, animal sacrifice and slave trade both existed. Indeed, they have existed since time immemorial.

Text 21

Translation
Thereafter, the famous King HariShcandra, one of the exalted persons in history, performed grand sacrifices by sacrificing a man and pleased all the demigods. In this way his dropsy created by Varuna was cured.

Text 22

Translation
In that great human sacrifice, ViShvamitra was the chief priest to offer oblations, the perfectly self-realized Jamadagni had the responsibility for chanting the mantras from the Yajur Veda, Vasishtha was the chief brahminical priest, and the sage Ayasya was the reciter of the hymns of the Sama Veda.

Text 23

Translation
King Indra, being very pleased with HariShcandra, offered him a gift of a golden chariot. ShunahShepha's glories will be presented along with the description of the son of ViShvamitra.

Text 24

Translation
The great sage ViShvamitra saw that Maharaja HariShcandra, along with his wife, was truthful, forbearing and concerned with the essence. Thus he gave them imperishable knowledge for fulfillment of the human mission.

Texts 25-26

Translation
Maharaja HariShcandra first purified his mind, which was full of material enjoyment, by amalgamating it with the earth. Then he amalgamated the earth with water, the water with fire, the fire with the air, and the air with the sky. Thereafter, he amalgamated the sky with the total material energy, and the total material energy with spiritual knowledge. This spiritual knowledge is realization of one's self as part of the Supreme Lord. When the self-realized spiritual soul is engaged in service to the Lord, he is eternally imperceptible and inconceivable. Thus established in spiritual knowledge, he is completely freed from material bondage.

Purport
Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Ninth Canto, Seventh Chapter, of the Shrimad-Bhagavatam, entitled “The Descendants of King Mandhata.”
