CHAPTER SIXTEEN
Lord ParaShurama Destroys the World's Ruling Class

When Jamadagni was killed by the sons of Kartaviryarjuna, as described in this chapter, ParaShurama rid the entire world of kshatriyas twenty-one times. This chapter also describes the descendants of ViShvamitra.

When Jamadagni's wife, Renuka, went to bring water from the Ganges and saw the King of the Gandharvas enjoying the company of Apsaras, she was captivated, and she slightly desired to associate with him. Because of this sinful desire, she was punished by her husband. ParaShurama killed his mother and brothers, but later, by dint of the austerities of Jamadagni, they were revived. The sons of Kartaviryarjuna, however, remembering the death of their father, wanted to take revenge against Lord ParaShurama, and therefore when ParaShurama was absent from the aShrama, they killed Jamadagni, who was meditating on the Supreme Personality of Godhead. When ParaShurama returned to the aShrama and saw his father killed, he was very sorry, and after asking his brothers to take care of the dead body, he went out with determination to kill all the kshatriyas on the surface of the world. Taking up his axe, he went to Mahishmati-pura, the capital of Kartaviryarjuna, and killed all of Kartaviryarjuna's sons, whose blood became a great river. ParaShurama, however, was not satisfied with killing only the sons of Kartaviryarjuna; later, when the kshatriyas became disturbing, he killed them twenty-one times, so that there were no kshatriyas on the surface of the earth. Thereafter, ParaShurama joined the head of his father to the dead body and performed various sacrifices to please the Supreme Lord. Thus Jamadagni got life again in his body, and later he was promoted to the higher planetary system known as Saptarshi-mandala. ParaShurama, the son of Jamadagni, still lives in Mahendra-parvata. In the next manvantara, he will become a preacher of Vedic knowledge.

In the dynasty of Gadhi, the most powerful ViShvamitra took birth. By dint of his austerity and penance, he became a brahmana. He had 101 sons, who were celebrated as the Madhucchandas. In the sacrificial arena of HariShcandra, the son of Ajigarta named ShunahShepha was meant to be sacrificed, but by the mercy of the Prajapatis he was released. Thereafter, he became Devarata in the dynasty of Gadhi. The fifty elder sons of ViShvamitra, however, did not accept ShunahShepha as their elder brother, and therefore ViShvamitra cursed them to become mlecchas, unfaithful to the Vedic civilization. ViShvamitra's fifty-first son, along with his younger brothers, then accepted ShunahShepha as their eldest brother, and their father, ViShvamitra, being satisfied, blessed them. Thus Devarata was accepted in the dynasty of KauShika, and consequently there are different divisions of that dynasty.

Text 1

Translation
Shukadeva Gosvami said: My dear Maharaja Parikshit, son of the Kuru dynasty, when Lord ParaShurama was given this order by his father, he immediately agreed, saying, “Let it be so.” For one complete year he traveled to holy places. Then he returned to his father's residence.

Text 2

Translation
Once when Renuka, the wife of Jamadagni, went to the bank of the Ganges to get water, she saw the King of the Gandharvas, decorated with a garland of lotuses and sporting in the Ganges with celestial women [Apsaras].

Text 3

Translation
She had gone to bring water from the Ganges, but when she saw Citraratha, the King of the Gandharvas, sporting with the celestial girls, she was somewhat inclined toward him and failed to remember that the time for the fire sacrifice was passing.

Text 4

Translation
Later, understanding that the time for offering the sacrifice had passed, Renuka feared a curse from her husband. Therefore when she returned she simply put the waterpot before him and stood there with folded hands.

Text 5

Translation
The great sage Jamadagni understood the adultery in the mind of his wife. Therefore he was very angry and told his sons, “My dear sons, kill this sinful woman!” But the sons did not carry out his order.

Text 6

Translation
Jamadagni then ordered his youngest son, ParaShurama, to kill his brothers, who had disobeyed this order, and his mother, who had mentally committed adultery. Lord ParaShurama, knowing the power of his father, who was practiced in meditation and austerity, killed his mother and brothers immediately.

Purport
The word prabhava-jnah is significant. ParaShurama knew the prowess of his father, and therefore he agreed to carry out his father's order. He thought that if he refused to carry out the order he would be cursed, but if he carried it out his father would be pleased, and when his father was pleased, ParaShurama would ask the benediction of having his mother and brothers brought back to life. ParaShurama was confident in this regard, and therefore he agreed to kill his mother and brothers.

Text 7

Translation
Jamadagni, the son of Satyavati, was very much pleased with ParaShurama and asked him to take any benediction he liked. Lord ParaShurama replied, “Let my mother and brothers live again and not remember having been killed by me. This is the benediction I ask.”

Text 8

Translation
Thereafter, by the benediction of Jamadagni, Lord ParaShurama's mother and brothers immediately came alive and were very happy, as if awakened from sound sleep. Lord ParaShurama had killed his relatives in accordance with his father's order because he was fully aware of his father's power, austerity and learning.

Text 9

Translation
My dear King Parikshit, the sons of Kartaviryarjuna, who were defeated by the superior strength of ParaShurama, never achieved happiness, for they always remembered the killing of their father.

Purport
Jamadagni was certainly very powerful due to his austerities, but because of a slight offense by his poor wife, Renuka, he ordered that she be killed. This certainly was a sinful act, and therefore Jamadagni was killed by the sons of Kartaviryarjuna, as described herein. Lord ParaShurama was also infected by sin because of killing Kartaviryarjuna, although this was not very offensive. Therefore, whether one be Kartaviryarjuna, Lord ParaShurama, Jamadagni or whoever one may be, one must act very cautiously and sagaciously; otherwise one must suffer the results of sinful activities. This is the lesson we receive from Vedic literature.

Text 10

Translation
Once when ParaShurama left the aShrama for the forest with Vasuman and his other brothers, the sons of Kartaviryarjuna took the opportunity to approach Jamadagni's residence to seek vengeance for their grudge.

Text 11

Translation
The sons of Kartaviryarjuna were determined to commit sinful deeds. Therefore when they saw Jamadagni sitting by the side of the fire to perform yajna and meditating upon the Supreme Personality of Godhead, who is praised by the best of selected prayers, they took the opportunity to kill him.

Text 12

Translation
With pitiable prayers, Renuka, the mother of ParaShurama and wife of Jamadagni, begged for the life of her husband. But the sons of Kartaviryarjuna, being devoid of the qualities of kshatriyas, were so cruel that despite her prayers they forcibly cut off his head and took it away.

Text 13

Translation
Lamenting in grief for the death of her husband, the most chaste Renuka struck her own body with her hands and cried very loudly, “O Rama, my dear son Rama!”

Text 14

Translation
Although the sons of Jamadagni, including Lord ParaShurama, were a long distance from home, as soon as they heard Renuka loudly calling “O Rama, O my son,” they hastily returned to the aShrama, where they saw their father already killed.

Text 15

Translation
Virtually bewildered by grief, anger, indignation, affliction and lamentation, the sons of Jamadagni cried, “O father, most religious, saintly person, you have left us and gone to the heavenly planets!”

Text 16

Translation
Thus lamenting, Lord ParaShurama entrusted his father's dead body to his brothers and personally took up his axe, having decided to put an end to all the kshatriyas on the surface of the world.

Text 17

Translation
O King, Lord ParaShurama then went to Mahishmati, which was already doomed by the sinful killing of a brahmana. In the midst of that city he made a mountain of heads, severed from the bodies of the sons of Kartaviryarjuna.

Texts 18-19

Translation
With the blood of the bodies of these sons, Lord ParaShurama created a ghastly river, which brought great fear to the kings who had no respect for brahminical culture. Because the kshatriyas, the men of power in government, were performing sinful activities, Lord ParaShurama, on the plea of retaliating for the murder of his father, rid all the kshatriyas from the face of the earth twenty-one times. Indeed, in the place known as Samanta-pancaka he created nine lakes filled with their blood.

Purport
ParaShurama is the Supreme Personality of Godhead, and his eternal mission is paritranaya sadhunam vinaShaya ca dushkrtam — to protect the devotees and annihilate the miscreants. To kill all the sinful men is one among the tasks of the incarnation of Godhead. Lord ParaShurama killed all the kshatriyas twenty-one times consecutively because they were disobedient to the brahminical culture. That the kshatriyas had killed his father was only a plea; the real fact is that because the kshatriyas, the ruling class, had become polluted, their position was inauspicious. Brahminical culture is enjoined in the Shastra, especially in Bhagavad-gita (catur-varnyam maya srshtam guna-karma-vibhagaShah). According to the laws of nature, whether at the time of ParaShurama or at the present, if the government becomes irresponsible and sinful, not caring for brahminical culture, there will certainly be an incarnation of God like ParaShurama to create a devastation by fire, famine, pestilence or some other calamity. Whenever the government disrespects the supremacy of the Personality of Godhead and fails to protect the institution of varnaShrama-dharma, it will certainly have to face such catastrophes as formerly brought about by Lord ParaShurama.

Text 20

Translation
Thereafter, ParaShurama joined his father's head to the dead body and placed the whole body and head upon kuSha grass. By offering sacrifices, he began to worship Lord Vasudeva, who is the all-pervading Supersoul of all the demigods and of every living entity.

Texts 21-22

Translation
After completing the sacrifice, Lord ParaShurama gave the eastern direction to the hota as a gift, the south to the brahma, the west to the adhvaryu, the north to the udgata, and the four corners — northeast, southeast, northwest and southwest — to the other priests. He gave the middle to KaShyapa and the place known as aryavarta to the upadrashta. Whatever remained he distributed among the sadasyas, the associate priests.

Purport
The tract of land in India between the Himalaya Mountains and the Vindhya Hills is called aryavarta.

Text 23

Translation
Thereafter, having completed the ritualistic sacrificial ceremonies, Lord ParaShurama took the bath known as the avabhrtha-snana. Standing on the bank of the great river Sarasvati, cleared of all sins, Lord ParaShurama appeared like the sun in a clear, cloudless sky.

Purport
As stated in Bhagavad-gita (3.9), yajnarthat karmano 'nyatra loko 'yam karma-bandhanah: “Work done as a sacrifice for Vishnu has to be performed; otherwise work binds one to this material world.” Karma-bandhanah refers to the repeated acceptance of one material body after another. The whole problem of life is this repetition of birth and death. Therefore one is advised to work to perform yajna meant for satisfying Lord Vishnu. Although Lord ParaShurama was an incarnation of the Supreme Personality of Godhead, he had to account for sinful activities. Anyone in this material world, however careful he may be, must commit some sinful activities, even though he does not want to. For example, one may trample many small ants and other insects while walking on the street and kill many living beings unknowingly. Therefore the Vedic principle of panca-yajna, five kinds of recommended sacrifice, is compulsory. In this Age of Kali, however, there is a great concession given to people in general. Yajnaih sankirtana-prayair yajanti hi sumedhasah: we may worship Lord Caitanya, the hidden incarnation of Krishna. Krishna-varnam tvishaKrishnam: although He is Krishna Himself, He always chants Hare Krishna and preaches Krishna consciousness. One is recommended to worship this incarnation by chanting, the sankirtana-yajna. The performance of sankirtana-yajna is a special concession for human society to save people from being affected by known or unknown sinful activities. We are surrounded by unlimited sins, and therefore it is compulsory that one take to Krishna consciousness and chant the Hare Krishna maha-mantra.

Text 24

Translation
Thus Jamadagni, being worshiped by Lord ParaShurama, was brought back to life with full remembrance, and he became one of the seven sages in the group of seven stars.

Purport
The seven stars revolving around the polestar at the zenith are called saptarshi-mandala. On these seven stars, which form the topmost part of our planetary system, reside seven sages: KaShyapa, Atri, Vasishtha, ViShvamitra, Gautama, Jamadagni and Bharadvaja. These seven stars are seen every night, and they each make a complete orbit around the polestar within twenty-four hours. Along with these seven stars, all the others stars also orbit from east to west. The upper portion of the universe is called the north, and the lower portion is called the south. Even in our ordinary dealings, while studying a map, we regard the upper portion of the map as north.

Text 25

Translation
My dear King Parikshit, in the next manvantara the lotus-eyed Personality of Godhead Lord ParaShurama, the son of Jamadagni, will be a great propounder of Vedic knowledge. In other words, he will be one of the seven sages.

Text 26

Translation
Lord ParaShurama still lives as an intelligent brahmana in the mountainous country known as Mahendra. Completely satisfied, having given up all the weapons of a kshatriya, he is always worshiped, adored and offered prayers for his exalted character and activities by such celestial beings as the Siddhas, Caranas and Gandharvas.

Text 27

Translation
In this way the supreme soul, the Supreme Personality of Godhead, the Lord and the supreme controller, descended as an incarnation in the Bhrgu dynasty and released the universe from the burden of undesirable kings by killing them many times.

Text 28

Translation
ViShvamitra, the son of Maharaja Gadhi, was as powerful as the flames of fire. From the position of a kshatriya, he achieved the position of a powerful brahmana by undergoing penances and austerities.

Purport
Now, having narrated the history of Lord ParaShurama, Shukadeva Gosvami begins the history of ViShvamitra. From the history of ParaShurama we can understand that although ParaShurama belonged to the brahminical group, he circumstantially had to work as a kshatriya. Later, after finishing his work as a kshatriya, he again became a brahmana and returned to Mahendra-parvata. Similarly, we can see that although ViShvamitra was born in a kshatriya family, by austerities and penances he achieved the position of a brahmana. These histories confirm the statements in Shastra that a brahmana may become a kshatriya, a kshatriya may become a brahmana or vaiShya, and a vaiShya may become a brahmana, by achieving the required qualities. One's status does not depend upon birth. As confirmed in Shrimad-Bhagavatam (7.11.35) by Narada:

yasya yal lakshanam proktam
pumso varnabhivyanjakam
yad anyatrapi drShyeta
tat tenaiva vinirdiShet

“If one shows the symptoms of being a brahmana, kshatriya, vaiShya or Shudra, even if he has appeared in a different class, he should be accepted according to those symptoms of classification.” To know who is a brahmana and who is a kshatriya, one must consider a man's quality and work. If all the unqualified Shudras become so-called brahmanas and kshatriyas, social order will be impossible to maintain. Thus there will be discrepancies, human society will turn into a society of animals, and the situation all over the world will be hellish.

Text 29

Translation
O King Parikshit, ViShvamitra had 101 sons, of whom the middle one was known as Madhucchanda. In relation to him, all the other sons were celebrated as the Madhucchandas.

Purport
In this connection, Shrila ViShvanatha Cakravarti thakura quotes this statement from the Vedas: tasya ha viShvamitrasyaika-Shatam putra asuh pancaShad eva jyayamso madhucchandasah pancaShat kaniyamsah. “ViShvamitra had 101 sons. Fifty were older than Madhucchanda and fifty younger.”

Text 30

Translation
ViShvamitra accepted the son of Ajigarta known as ShunahShepha, who was born in the Bhrgu dynasty and was also known as Devarata, as one of his own sons. ViShvamitra ordered his other sons to accept ShunahShepha as their eldest brother.

Text 31

Translation
ShunahShepha's father sold ShunahShepha to be sacrificed as a man-animal in the yajna of King HariShcandra. When ShunahShepha was brought into the sacrificial arena, he prayed to the demigods for release and was released by their mercy.

Purport
Here is a description of ShunahShepha. When HariShcandra was to sacrifice his son Rohita, Rohita arranged to save his own life by purchasing ShunahShepha from ShunahShepha's father to be sacrificed in the yajna. ShunahShepha was sold to Maharaja HariShcandra because he was the middle son, between the oldest and the youngest. It appears that the sacrifice of a man as an animal in yajna has been practiced for a very long time.

Text 32

Translation
Although ShunahShepha was born in the Bhargava dynasty, he was greatly advanced in spiritual life, and therefore the demigods involved in the sacrifice protected him. Consequently he was also celebrated as the descendant of Gadhi named Devarata.

Text 33

Translation
When requested by their father to accept ShunahShepha as the eldest son, the elder fifty of the Madhucchandas, the sons of ViShvamitra, did not agree. Therefore ViShvamitra, being angry, cursed them. “May all of you bad sons become mlecchas,” he said, “being opposed to the principles of Vedic culture.”

Purport
In Vedic literature there are names like mleccha and yavana. The mlecchas are understood to be those who do not follow the Vedic principles. In former days, the mlecchas were fewer, and ViShvamitra Muni cursed his sons to become mlecchas. But in the present age, Kali-yuga, there is no need of cursing, for people are automatically mlecchas. This is only the beginning of Kali-yuga, but at the end of Kali-yuga the entire population will consist of mlecchas because no one will follow the Vedic principles. At that time the incarnation Kalki will appear. Mleccha-nivaha-nidhane kalayasi karavalam. He will kill all the mlecchas indiscriminately with his sword.

Text 34

Translation
When the elder Madhucchandas were cursed, the younger fifty, along with Madhucchanda himself, approached their father and agreed to accept his proposal. “Dear father,” they said, “we shall abide by whatever arrangement you like.”

Text 35

Translation
Thus the younger Madhucchandas accepted ShunahShepha as their eldest brother and told him, “We shall follow your orders.” ViShvamitra then said to his obedient sons, “Because you have accepted ShunahShepha as your eldest brother, I am very satisfied. By accepting my order, you have made me a father of worthy sons, and therefore I bless all of you to become the fathers of sons also.”

Purport
Of the one hundred sons, half disobeyed ViShvamitra by not accepting ShunahShepha as their eldest brother, but the other half accepted his order. Therefore the father blessed the obedient sons to become the fathers of sons. Otherwise they too would have been cursed to be sonless mlecchas.

Text 36

Translation
ViShvamitra said, “O KuShikas [descendants of KauShika], this Devarata is my son and is one of you. Please obey his orders.” O King Parikshit, ViShvamitra had many other sons, such as Ashtaka, Harita, Jaya and Kratuman.

Text 37

Translation
ViShvamitra cursed some of his sons and blessed the others, and he also adopted a son. Thus there were varieties in the KauShika dynasty, but among all the sons, Devarata was considered the eldest.

Purport
Thus end the Bhaktivedanta purports of the Ninth Canto, Sixteenth Chapter, of the Shrimad-Bhagavatam, entitled “Lord ParaShurama Destroys the World's Ruling Class.”
